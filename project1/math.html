Math is the Original Game

Math is often associated with pain, discomfort, embarrassment, or shame. I feel, however, that math is a creative practice.

Exploring math should be as painless and joyful as exploring ceramics.

Similar to other art forms with an open ended nature, the open questions in math give us much room to play.

Math is a form of artistic expression, and should be as pleasurable to explore over an empty weekend as oil painting.

Math is often associated with pain, discomfort, embarrassment, or shame because there are right and wrong answers.

Exploring math is often not as painless and joyful as exploring ceramics because of internal or external pressure to get the right answer as quickly possible.

This pressure to get answers right in a public setting turns many away from math before they even discover the fertile ground for exploration and personal growth.
